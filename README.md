# agile-sprint-review
Portfolio repository for Computer Science coursework featuring Agile and Scrum practices
How do I interpret user needs and implement them into a program? How does creating “user stories” help with this?
I interpret user needs by breaking down overarching goals into clear, functional requirements. Creating "user stories" is essential in this process because it frames technical tasks strictly from the perspective of the end-user (e.g., "As a user, I want to [action] so that [benefit]"). This practice ensures that the development team stays focused on delivering actual value, rather than just writing code, and keeps the final program aligned with its intended real-world application.

How do I approach developing programs? What Agile processes do I hope to incorporate into my future development work?
My approach to developing programs revolves around iterative planning, continuous testing, and adaptability. Rather than attempting to build an entire system at once, I focus on developing functional, bite-sized increments. In future development work, I hope to consistently incorporate Scrum frameworks—particularly sprint planning and retrospectives. These processes provide built-in opportunities to evaluate progress, adapt to changing requirements, and continuously improve both the product and the team's workflow.

What does it mean to be a good team member in software development?
Being a good team member in software development means communicating clearly, being receptive to feedback, and actively supporting your peers. Beyond just completing your assigned tasks during a sprint, it involves writing clean, well-documented code that others can easily understand and maintain. It also means participating constructively in code reviews and being willing to help troubleshoot when teammates encounter blockers.
